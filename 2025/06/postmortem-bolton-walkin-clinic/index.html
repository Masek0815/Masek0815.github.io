PostMortem: Bolton Walkin Clinic

June 29, 2025

Type of leak

RSYNC server (99.249.12.209) exposing a data backup from a clinic. Thousands of medical documents including the PII of the patients can be accessed without any username or password.

Threats from the leak

I see the following threats:

  • Confidentiality people seeking medical help is compromised
  • Privacy of Canadian citizens compromised
  • Very likely violation of several laws

Timeline

  • August 2024: Leak is identified by a security researcher
  • Security researcher tries to call and email the clinic and gets rebuffed. Even after a public disclosure, there is no reaction.
  • June 3rd, 2025: I reachout to the ISP (Rogers Communications Inc) as the publication of that data is in clear violation of the acceptable use policy of Rogers.
  • June 3rd, 2025: Rogers asks for proof of the claim which is supplied the same day.
  • June 12th, 2025: Contacted the Ontario Provincial Police and asked for an investigation if Canadian law is being violated.
  • June 18th, 2025: Ontario Provincial Police passes the investigation to the Anti-Rackets Branch.
  • June 20th, 2025: Passed the information about the investigation to Rogers Communication.
  • June 21st, 2025: Leak is closed

Analysis

This is not a complete failure analysis. This are only my observations. A full detailed analysis is most likely to be even more shocking.

Failures:

  • The leak looked like an unecrypted backup. That is a big “no no” in case of medical data.
  • The RSYNC server was not protected at all (default port, no username/password required).
  • Even though the origin of the data was easily identified, the Bolton Walin Clinic was unable to react to informations about the leak.
  • SUMMARY: A complete and utter failure of IT-Security on the technical and organisational level of the Bolton Walkin Clinic

Impact

It can be safely assumed (due to duration and easiness to discover) that all data on those server is now in the hands of inttelligence services (e.g. USA, Russia, China) with little care about the privacy of Canadian citizens.

Acknowledgments

Thanks to JayeLTee and Dissent Doe for working a long time on that leak.

Furthermore I wish to thank the Abuse-team of Rogers Communication and Ontario Provincial Police.

Also thanks to the Canadian Centre for Cyber Security who also tried to get into contact with the Clinic but was ignored as well by them.

Closing Remarks

It is clearly necessary that we have at least one public contact in each country that investigates and closes data leaks reported to them. The effort to close even the worst leaks is unbearable and currently rests on the shoulders of security researchers and their supporting environment.

Time spent on this leak from my side (without the time for this report) is 6+ hours. My best estimate on the effort of all people involved closing this leak would be in the hundreds of hours. The amount of time spent by the person responsible for the leaking system on security issues: None.
