Day 78: Choosing a bike

June 6, 2020

This article is part of The 100 Days Offensive. Go to Day 77 or Day 79.

After my bike was stolen, I organized myself a temporary replacement. But it will be temporary, there is too much that doesn’t really work for me.

So with the help of a lot of friends (online and offline) I looked into what I (don’t) want from a bicycle. The list has become rather long.

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS:

  • I don’t drive much, rarely more than 15 miles per day. As my joints are currently still in working condition, I decided against a pedelec.
  • Comfort before speed: I want to enjoy riding that bike, not arrive in the least possible time.
  • I’ll be riding it in the city on streets and bike lanes, not off-road. My state is about as flat as a pancake. Hills with an elevation of 50 yards are already called mountain. So I do not need a lot of gears.
  • Low maintenance is a very important aspect. I completely lack manual skills and hate fixing things. I’m willing to give a bicycle to the service once a year and pay for it, but in the remaining time I expect to “just work”.
  • I’m not price sensitive. The last one lasted 20 years, so cost per year has been unbeatable low.
  • With a weight of 300 pounds, I’m quite heavy and the bike must be able to accept that and extra load (e.g. 40 pounds of purchases). The height is 6 feet, but I have rather short legs in proportion. Last time I picked a frame for 28’’ wheels, that was too high and prevented a saddle suspension.
  • When I see other cyclist, I feel a bit like an energizer bunny. My comfort zone is at about 80 RPM which seems higher than with others.
  • With the last bike I had a rather forward leaning position and a pretzel shaped handlebar. Due to the advancing age, I want to sit more upright on the next one.
  • I want to lease the new one.

MY PREFERENCES:

  • Suspension for an extra wide saddle and the front
  • Carrier for basket and saddle bags
  • 7-10 gears (e.g. Shimano Nexus)
  • Dealer/service close by or on-site-service
  • Brakes: Low maintenance, all-weather
  • Wide wheel rims, high end tire (e.g. Schwalbe Marathon), scraper valve
  • Light: dynamo hub, parking light, high illumination level

DISLIKES:

  • Backpedal brake
  • Sclaverand valves
  • Carrier on the front wheel
  • Clipless pedal

WHAT AM I STILL THINKING ABOUT:

  • V-stand
  • Toothed belt transmission
